  1. Question

    The lengths (in centimeters) of 10 lizards were recorded.

    𝐱=83,89,87,85,83,89,86,87,86,88\mathbf{x} = 83, 89, 87, 85, 83, 89, 86, 87, 86, 88

    You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): lizard_data.csv


    1. What proportion of the measurements are inside 85.5 cm to 87.5 cm? In other words, determine prop[𝐱>85.5 𝙰𝙽𝙳 𝐱<87.5]\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>85.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<87.5]. In other words, determine prop[85.5<𝐱<87.5]\text{prop}[85.5<\mathbf{x}<87.5].
    2. What proportion of the measurements are outside 80.5 cm to 85.5 cm? In other words, determine prop[𝐱<80.5 𝙾𝚁 𝐱>85.5]\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<80.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>85.5]. In other words, determine prop[𝙽𝙾𝚃(80.5<𝐱<85.5)]\text{prop}[\texttt{NOT}(80.5<\mathbf{x}<85.5)].
    3. What proportion of the measurements are closer than 4.5 cm from 85 cm? In other words, determine prop[|𝐱−85|<4.5]\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-85\big|<4.5\right].
    4. What proportion of the measurements are farther than 0.5 cm from 83 cm? In other words, determine prop[|𝐱−83|>0.5]\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-83\big|>0.5\right].
    5. Determine a half-integer radius rr of an interval with center 83 cm such that prop[|𝐱−83|<r]=0.3\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-83\big|<r\right] ~=~ 0.3.
    6. Determine an integer radius rr of an interval with center 87.5 cm such that prop[|𝐱−87.5|>r]=0.7\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-87.5\big|>r\right] ~=~ 0.7.

    Solution

    First, it helps to sort the data. sort(𝐱)=83,83,85,86,86,87,87,88,89,89\text{sort}(\mathbf{x}) = 83, 83, 85, 86, 86, 87, 87, 88, 89, 89

    You could use R to sort the data:

    sort(c(83,89,87,85,83,89,86,87,86,88))
    ##  [1] 83 83 85 86 86 87 87 88 89 89

    You could also use a spreadsheet. Import the table into a spreadsheet. Then, highlight the column of measurements and use Sort function.


    1. The proportion of measurements between 85.5 and 87.5 is 0.4. prop[85.5<𝐱<87.5]=0.4\text{prop}[85.5<\mathbf{x}<87.5]=0.4
    2. The proportion of measurements outside 80.5 and 85.5 is 0.7. prop[𝙽𝙾𝚃(80.5<𝐱<85.5)]=0.7\text{prop}[\texttt{NOT}(80.5<\mathbf{x}<85.5)]=0.7
    3. The proportion of measurements closer than 4.5 cm from 85 cm is 1. prop[|𝐱−85|<4.5]=1\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-85\big|<4.5\right] = 1
    4. The proportion of measurements farther than 0.5 cm from 83 cm is 0.8. prop[|𝐱−83|>0.5]=0.8\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-83\big|>0.5\right] = 0.8
    5. You can guess and check until you find a half-integer that satisfies the equation. It turns out that r=2.5r = 2.5 satisfies the equation, because prop[|𝐱−83|<2.5]=0.3\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-83\big|<2.5\right] ~=~ 0.3
    6. You can guess and check until you find an integer that satisfies the equation. It turns out that r=1r = 1 satisfies the equation, because prop[|𝐱−87.5|>1]=0.7\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-87.5\big|>1\right] ~=~ 0.7
