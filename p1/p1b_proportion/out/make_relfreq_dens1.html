  1. Question

    A random procedure generated n=100n=100 measurements: download data

    I’ve already determined the frequencies. Please determine the relative frequencies and the densities. A brief description of relative frequency and density can be found here.

    Interval Frequency Relative Frequency Density
    35 to 40 3
    40 to 45 2
    45 to 50 6
    50 to 55 23
    55 to 60 66


    Solution

    To determine the relative frequencies, just divide each frequency by 100 (because n=100n=100). To determine the densities, divide the relative frequencies by the width of the interval, which in this case is the same for each interval (40−35=540-35=5).

    Interval Frequency Relative Frequency Density
    35 to 40 3 0.03 0.006
    40 to 45 2 0.02 0.004
    45 to 50 6 0.06 0.012
    50 to 55 23 0.23 0.046
    55 to 60 66 0.66 0.132
