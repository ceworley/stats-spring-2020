  1. Question

    A standard eight-sided die was rolled 50 times, and the results were organized into the histogram shown below. In dice notation, we could say the results of 50d8 were plotted as a histogram. (Pedantic sidenote: it is common to interpret 50d8 as the SUM of 50 rolls, but we will interpret 50d8 as the LIST of 50 rolls and use ∑50d8\sum 50\text{d}8 as the sum of 50 rolls.)



    1. Evaluate prop[𝐱<7.5]\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<7.5].
    2. Evaluate prop[𝐱>1.5]\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>1.5].
    3. Evaluate prop[|𝐱−5|<1.5]\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-5|<1.5].
    4. Evaluate prop[|𝐱−5|>1.5]\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-5|>1.5].
    5. Evaluate half-integer boundary bb such that prop[𝐱<b]=0.28\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<b] = 0.28.
    6. Evaluate half-integer boundary bb such that prop[𝐱>b]=0.58\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>b] = 0.58.
    7. Evaluate integer radius rr such that prop[|𝐱−6.5|<r]=0.36\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-6.5|<r] = 0.36.
    8. Evaluate half-integer radius rr such that prop[|𝐱−6|>r]=0.56\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-6|>r] = 0.56.

    Solution

    The first 4 questions involve adding up relative frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.

    You may also find it is helpful to determine frequencies (counts). You do this by multiplying each relative frequency by the total number of measurements.



    1. There are 39 measurements less than 7.5. So, prop[𝐱<7.5]=3950=0.78\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<7.5]=\frac{39}{50}=0.78.
    2. There are 44 measurements more than 1.5. So, prop[𝐱>1.5]=4450=0.88\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>1.5]=\frac{44}{50}=0.88.
    3. There are 15 measurements closer than 1.5 units from 5. So, prop[|𝐱−5|<1.5]=1550=0.3\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-5|<1.5]=\frac{15}{50}=0.3.
    4. There are 35 measurements further than 1.5 units from 5. So, prop[|𝐱−5|>1.5]=3550=0.7\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-5|>1.5]=\frac{35}{50}=0.7.
    5. First, convert the proportion into a count. 0.28⋅50=140.28\cdot50=14. A boundary at b=3.5b=3.5 has 14 measurements less than it. So, prop[𝐱<3.5]=0.28\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<3.5]=0.28.
    6. First, convert the proportion into a count. 0.58⋅50=290.58\cdot50=29. A boundary at b=5.5b=5.5 has 29 measurements more than it. So, prop[𝐱>5.5]=0.58\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>5.5]=0.58.
    7. An interval with radius r=1r=1 around center 6.5 contains 18 measurements. So, prop[|𝐱−6.5|<1]=0.36\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-6.5|<1]=0.36.
    8. An interval with radius r=1.5r=1.5 around center 6 excludes 28 measurements. So, prop[|𝐱−6|>1.5]=0.56\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-6|>1.5]=0.56.
