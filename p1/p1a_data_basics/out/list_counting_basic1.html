  1. Question

    A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

    𝐱=83,83,89,81,86,85,84,87,80,80\mathbf{x} = 83, 83, 89, 81, 86, 85, 84, 87, 80, 80

    You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_basic_data_34541.csv


    1. How many of the measurements are less than 80? In other words, determine #[𝐱<80]\#[\mathbf{x}<80].
    2. How many of the measurements are less than or equal to 83? In other words, determine #[𝐱≤83]\#[\mathbf{x}\le83].
    3. How many of the measurements are greater than 89? In other words, determine #[𝐱>89]\#[\mathbf{x}>89].
    4. How many of the measurements are greater than or equal to 81? In other words, determine #[𝐱≥81]\#[\mathbf{x}\ge81].
    5. If 3 of the measurements are less than xcx_{\text{c}}, and xcx_{\text{c}} is a measurement from sample 𝐱\mathbf{x}, then what is xcx_{\text{c}}? In other words, determine a measurement xcx_{\text{c}} from sequence 𝐱\mathbf{x} such that #[𝐱<xc]=3\#[\mathbf{x}<x_{\text{c}}] = 3.
    6. If 8 of the measurements are less than or equal to xcx_{\text{c}}, and xcx_{\text{c}} is a measurement from sample 𝐱\mathbf{x}, then what is xcx_{\text{c}}? In other words, determine a measurement xcx_{\text{c}} from sequence 𝐱\mathbf{x} such that #[𝐱≤xc]=8\#[\mathbf{x}\le x_{\text{c}}] = 8.
    7. If 8 of the measurements are greater than xcx_{\text{c}}, and xcx_{\text{c}} is a measurement from sample 𝐱\mathbf{x}, then what is xcx_{\text{c}}? In other words, determine a measurement xcx_{\text{c}} from sequence 𝐱\mathbf{x} such that #[𝐱>xc]=8\#[\mathbf{x}>x_{\text{c}}] = 8.
    8. If 5 of the measurements are greater than or equal to xcx_{\text{c}}, and xcx_{\text{c}} is a measurement from sample 𝐱\mathbf{x}, then what is xcx_{\text{c}}? In other words, determine a measurement xcx_{\text{c}} from sequence 𝐱\mathbf{x} such that #[𝐱≥xc]=5\#[\mathbf{x}\ge x_{\text{c}}] = 5.

    Solution

    First, it helps to sort the data. sort(𝐱)=80,80,81,83,83,84,85,86,87,89\text{sort}(\mathbf{x}) = 80, 80, 81, 83, 83, 84, 85, 86, 87, 89

    You could use R to sort the data:

    sort(c(83,83,89,81,86,85,84,87,80,80))
    ##  [1] 80 80 81 83 83 84 85 86 87 89

    You could also use a spreadsheet. Import the table into a spreadsheet. Then, highlight the column of measurements and use Sort function.


    1. There are 0 measurements less than 80.
    2. There are 5 measurements less than or equal to 83.
    3. There are 0 measurements greater than 89.
    4. There are 8 measurements greater than or equal to 81.
    5. Measurement value 83 has 3 measurements less than it, so xc=83x_{\text{c}}=83.
    6. Measurement value 86 has 8 measurements less than or equal to it, so xc=86x_{\text{c}}=86.
    7. Measurement value 80 has 8 measurements greater than it, so xc=80x_{\text{c}}=80.
    8. Measurement value 84 has 5 measurements greater than or equal to it, so xc=84x_{\text{c}}=84.
