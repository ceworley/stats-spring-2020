read_frequency_distribution

  1. Question

    A random procedure generated 60 measurements, which were organized into the frequency distribution shown below. You can assume the measurements are of a continuous random variable, such that every measurement is in one of the intervals (and not on a break).

    interval frequency
    60 to 65 10
    65 to 70 11
    70 to 75 15
    75 to 80 11
    80 to 85 5
    85 to 90 8

    1. Evaluate #[𝐱<75]\#[\mathbf{x}<75].
    2. Evaluate #[𝐱>80]\#[\mathbf{x}>80].
    3. Evaluate #[|𝐱−77.5|<7.5]\#[|\mathbf{x}-77.5|<7.5].
    4. Evaluate #[|𝐱−75|>5]\#[|\mathbf{x}-75|>5].
    5. Evaluate boundary bb such that #[𝐱<b]=36\#[\mathbf{x}<b] = 36.
    6. Evaluate boundary bb such that #[𝐱>b]=24\#[\mathbf{x}>b] = 24.
    7. Evaluate radius rr such that #[|𝐱−75|<r]=42\#[|\mathbf{x}-75|<r] = 42.
    8. Evaluate radius rr such that #[|𝐱−80|>r]=44\#[|\mathbf{x}-80|>r] = 44.

    Solution

    The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


    1. There are 36 measurements less than 75. So, #[𝐱<75]=36\#[\mathbf{x}<75]=36.
    2. There are 13 measurements more than 80. So, #[𝐱>80]=13\#[\mathbf{x}>80]=13.
    3. There are 31 measurements closer than 7.5 units from 77.5. So, #[|𝐱−77.5|<7.5]=31\#[|\mathbf{x}-77.5|<7.5]=31.
    4. There are 34 measurements further than 5 units from 75. So, #[|𝐱−75|>5]=34\#[|\mathbf{x}-75|>5]=34.
    5. A boundary at b=75b=75 has 36 measurements less than it. So, #[𝐱<75]=36\#[\mathbf{x}<75]=36.
    6. A boundary at b=75b=75 has 24 measurements more than it. So, #[𝐱>75]=24\#[\mathbf{x}>75]=24.
    7. An interval with radius r=10r=10 around center 75 contains 42 measurements. So, #[|𝐱−75|<10]=42\#[|\mathbf{x}-75|<10]=42.
    8. An interval with radius r=5r=5 around center 80 excludes 44 measurements. So, #[|𝐱−80|<5]=44\#[|\mathbf{x}-80|<5]=44.
