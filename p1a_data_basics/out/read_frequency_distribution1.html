read_frequency_distribution

  1. Question

    A random procedure generated 120 measurements, which were organized into the frequency distribution shown below. You can assume the measurements are of a continuous random variable, such that every measurement is in one of the intervals (and not on a break).

    interval frequency
    34 to 36 2
    36 to 38 3
    38 to 40 7
    40 to 42 9
    42 to 44 16
    44 to 46 16
    46 to 48 14
    48 to 50 53

    1. Evaluate #[𝐱<44]\#[\mathbf{x}<44].
    2. Evaluate #[𝐱>44]\#[\mathbf{x}>44].
    3. Evaluate #[|𝐱−40|<2]\#[|\mathbf{x}-40|<2].
    4. Evaluate #[|𝐱−40|>4]\#[|\mathbf{x}-40|>4].
    5. Evaluate boundary bb such that #[𝐱<b]=37\#[\mathbf{x}<b] = 37.
    6. Evaluate boundary bb such that #[𝐱>b]=67\#[\mathbf{x}>b] = 67.
    7. Evaluate radius rr such that #[|𝐱−43|<r]=16\#[|\mathbf{x}-43|<r] = 16.
    8. Evaluate radius rr such that #[|𝐱−44|>r]=65\#[|\mathbf{x}-44|>r] = 65.

    Solution

    The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


    1. There are 37 measurements less than 44. So, #[𝐱<44]=37\#[\mathbf{x}<44]=37.
    2. There are 83 measurements more than 44. So, #[𝐱>44]=83\#[\mathbf{x}>44]=83.
    3. There are 16 measurements closer than 2 units from 40. So, #[|𝐱−40|<2]=16\#[|\mathbf{x}-40|<2]=16.
    4. There are 85 measurements further than 4 units from 40. So, #[|𝐱−40|>4]=85\#[|\mathbf{x}-40|>4]=85.
    5. A boundary at b=44b=44 has 37 measurements less than it. So, #[𝐱<44]=37\#[\mathbf{x}<44]=37.
    6. A boundary at b=46b=46 has 67 measurements more than it. So, #[𝐱>46]=67\#[\mathbf{x}>46]=67.
    7. An interval with radius r=1r=1 around center 43 contains 16 measurements. So, #[|𝐱−43|<1]=16\#[|\mathbf{x}-43|<1]=16.
    8. An interval with radius r=4r=4 around center 44 excludes 65 measurements. So, #[|𝐱−44|<4]=65\#[|\mathbf{x}-44|<4]=65.
