list_counting_between

  1. Question

    A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

    sort(𝐱)=88,84,81,83,87,85,87,82,84,82\text{sort}(\mathbf{x}) = 88, 84, 81, 83, 87, 85, 87, 82, 84, 82

    You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_between_data_91484.csv


    1. How many of the measurements are inside 82.5 to 84.5? In other words, determine #[𝐱>82.5 𝙰𝙽𝙳 𝐱<84.5]\#[\mathbf{x}>82.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<84.5]. In other words, determine #[82.5<𝐱<84.5]\#[82.5<\mathbf{x}<84.5].
    2. How many of the measurements are outside 82.5 to 88.5? In other words, determine #[𝐱<82.5 𝙾𝚁 𝐱>88.5]\#[\mathbf{x}<82.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>88.5]. In other words, determine #[𝙽𝙾𝚃(82.5<𝐱<88.5)]\#[\texttt{NOT}(82.5<\mathbf{x}<88.5)].
    3. How many of the measurements are closer than 1 unit from 86.5? In other words, determine #[|𝐱−86.5|<1]\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-86.5\big|<1\right].
    4. How many of the measurements are farther than 1 unit from 82.5? In other words, determine #[|𝐱−82.5|>1]\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-82.5\big|>1\right].
    5. Determine a half-integer radius rr of an interval with center 83 such that #[|𝐱−83|<r]=5\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-83\big|<r\right] ~=~ 5.
    6. Determine a half-integer radius rr of an interval with center 84 such that #[|𝐱−84|>r]=4\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-84\big|>r\right] ~=~ 4.

    Solution

    First, it helps to sort the data. sort(𝐱)=81,82,82,83,84,84,85,87,87,88\text{sort}(\mathbf{x}) = 81, 82, 82, 83, 84, 84, 85, 87, 87, 88

    You could use R to sort the data:

    sort(c(88,84,81,83,87,85,87,82,84,82))
    ##  [1] 81 82 82 83 84 84 85 87 87 88

    You could also use a spreadsheet. Import the table into a spreadsheet. Then, highlight the column of measurements and use Sort function.


    1. There are 3 measurements between 82.5 and 84.5.
    2. There are 3 measurements outside 82.5 and 88.5.
    3. There are 2 measurements closer than 1 units from 86.5. It might help to rephrase the problem as finding how many measurements are between 85.5 and 87.5. These boundaries are found by subtracting 1 from 86.5 and adding 1 to 86.5.
    4. There are 3 measurements farther than 1 units from 82.5. It might help to rephrase the problem as finding how many measurements are outside 81.5 and 83.5. These boundaries are found by subtracting 1 from 82.5 and adding 1 to 82.5.
    5. You can guess and check until you find a half-integer that satisfies the equation. It turns out that r=1.5r = 1.5 satisfies the equation, because #[|𝐱−83|<1.5]=5\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-83\big|<1.5\right] ~=~ 5.
    6. You can guess and check until you find a half-integer that satisfies the equation. It turns out that r=2.5r = 2.5 satisfies the equation, because #[|𝐱−84|>2.5]=4\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-84\big|>2.5\right] ~=~ 4.
