    A standard ten-sided die was rolled 70 times, and the results were organized into the histogram shown below. In dice notation, we could say the results of 70d10 were plotted as a histogram. (Pedantic sidenote: it is common to interpret 70d10 as the SUM of 70 rolls, but we will interpret 70d10 as the LIST of 70 rolls and use âˆ‘70d10\sum 70\text{d}10 as the sum of 70 rolls.)

    2. Evaluate #[ğ±>4.5]\#[\mathbf{x}>4.5].

    Solution

    The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


    1. There are 42 measurements less than 6.5. So, #[ğ±<6.5]=42\#[\mathbf{x}<6.5]=42.
    2. There are 38 measurements more than 4.5. So, #[ğ±>4.5]=38\#[\mathbf{x}>4.5]=38.
    3. There are 4 measurements closer than 0.5 units from 5. So, #[|ğ±âˆ’5|<0.5]=4\#[|\mathbf{x}-5|<0.5]=4.
    4. There are 45 measurements further than 2 units from 5.5. So, #[|ğ±âˆ’5.5|>2]=45\#[|\mathbf{x}-5.5|>2]=45.
    5. A boundary at b=4.5b=4.5 has 32 measurements less than it. So, #[ğ±<4.5]=32\#[\mathbf{x}<4.5]=32.
    6. A boundary at b=7.5b=7.5 has 20 measurements more than it. So, #[ğ±>7.5]=20\#[\mathbf{x}>7.5]=20.
    7. An interval with radius r=2r=2 around center 6.5 contains 25 measurements. So, #[|ğ±âˆ’6.5|<2]=25\#[|\mathbf{x}-6.5|<2]=25.
    8. An interval with radius r=1.5r=1.5 around center 5 excludes 53 measurements. So, #[|ğ±âˆ’5|>1.5]=53\#[|\mathbf{x}-5|>1.5]=53.
