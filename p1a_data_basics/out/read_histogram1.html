  1. Question

    A random procedure generated 150 measurements, which were organized into the histogram shown below. You can assume the measurements are of a continuous variable, such that every measurement is in one of the intervals (and not on a break).



    1. Evaluate #[𝐱<38]\#[\mathbf{x}<38].
    2. Evaluate #[𝐱>40]\#[\mathbf{x}>40].
    3. Evaluate #[|𝐱−36|<2]\#[|\mathbf{x}-36|<2].
    4. Evaluate #[|𝐱−38|>4]\#[|\mathbf{x}-38|>4].
    5. Evaluate boundary bb such that #[𝐱<b]=85\#[\mathbf{x}<b] = 85.
    6. Evaluate boundary bb such that #[𝐱>b]=21\#[\mathbf{x}>b] = 21.
    7. Evaluate radius rr such that #[|𝐱−38|<r]=96\#[|\mathbf{x}-38|<r] = 96.
    8. Evaluate radius rr such that #[|𝐱−38|>r]=8\#[|\mathbf{x}-38|>r] = 8.

    Solution

    The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


    1. There are 85 measurements less than 38. So, #[𝐱<38]=85\#[\mathbf{x}<38]=85.
    2. There are 21 measurements more than 40. So, #[𝐱>40]=21\#[\mathbf{x}>40]=21.
    3. There are 81 measurements closer than 2 units from 36. So, #[|𝐱−36|<2]=81\#[|\mathbf{x}-36|<2]=81.
    4. There are 8 measurements further than 4 units from 38. So, #[|𝐱−38|>4]=8\#[|\mathbf{x}-38|>4]=8.
    5. A boundary at b=38b=38 has 85 measurements less than it. So, #[𝐱<38]=85\#[\mathbf{x}<38]=85.
    6. A boundary at b=40b=40 has 21 measurements more than it. So, #[𝐱>40]=21\#[\mathbf{x}>40]=21.
    7. An interval with radius r=2r=2 around center 38 contains 96 measurements. So, #[|𝐱−38|<2]=96\#[|\mathbf{x}-38|<2]=96.
    8. An interval with radius r=4r=4 around center 38 excludes 8 measurements. So, #[|𝐱−38|>4]=8\#[|\mathbf{x}-38|>4]=8.
